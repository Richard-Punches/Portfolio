Collaboration & Enablement

Stakeholder Management

Getting subject matter experts and business unit leads to adopt DITA and topic-based authoring isn't just a technical exercise, it's a people challenge. I've spent years helping teams that are skeptical of structured authoring discover why it works for them, not just for the documentation department.

Onboarding & Mentoring

I bring business units into structured authoring workflows through online mentoring sessions tailored to their level of familiarity. From initial concept walkthroughs to hands-on topic construction, I meet stakeholders where they are.

Multichannel Publishing Guidance

Once a team understands DITA's reuse potential, the next step is publishing. I walk stakeholders through multichannel output strategies; web, PDF, help systems — so their investment in structured content pays dividends across every channel.

DITA Tagging Reviews

I conduct ongoing structural reviews of in-progress DITA content, providing concrete, actionable feedback on topic architecture, element use, and metadata tagging before bad habits calcify.

Embracing the Skeptics

Some of the most rewarding work I've done is turning resistant stakeholders into DITA advocates. By focusing on their pain points first and structured authoring second, I've helped hesitant teams make the shift on their own terms.

DITA & XML Methodology

Structured Authoring

I've been doing structured authoring for years. Over a decade of hands-on work with the methodology has shaped how I approach every documentation project, whether it's a hardware product manual, an SOP library, a Salesforce knowledge module, or an enterprise help system.


The real value in structured authoring isn't the tagging, it's the thinking it demands. Topic-based authoring forces clarity about what a piece of content is actually for, which makes every deliverable more useful to its audience.


I have deep experience with content conditionalization for reuse, which involve building topic pools serving multiple products, audiences, and output formats from one source, reducing maintenance overhead and version drift.

Structure & Taxonomy

Information Architecture

Good information architecture is invisible when it works; content is where users expect it, metadata is consistent, and reuse happens without friction. I've contributed to enterprise-scale IA projects where those outcomes were far from guaranteed.

Enterprise CCMS Restructuring

Participated in a ground-up restructuring of an enterprise Component Content Management System (CCMS) to enable systematic content reuse. This involved auditing existing topic structures, identifying reuse opportunities, and implementing map architectures that reduced content duplication across product lines.

Multilingual Metadata Redesign

Took part in a cross-functional initiative to redesign multilingual metadata schemes, ensuring that taxonomy structures and classification attributes were consistent and translatable across language variants, a prerequisite for scalable localization workflows.

Content Conditionalization Frameworks

Designed and implemented conditional processing attribute schemes (DITAVAL) enabling a single topic pool to serve multiple product variants, audience levels, and delivery channels without content branching.

Intel Information Repository Management

Managed the information repository and system architecture for Intel modem and gateway product documentation, maintaining the integrity of topic relationships, map hierarchies, and metadata schemas across a large, actively-maintained content set.
